When deciding whether to add a service to GBP, build a service page, or write content for a query, **don't filter only by "is this Andrew's primary work."** Filter by **funnel value**: does this search intent often convert into a bigger job?

**Why:** Bill confirmed on 2026-04-30: *"lets add it because if someone searches that we want to come up cause its ultimtly a bigger job if they can convert it."* The example was "Electrical fuse changing" — Andrew rarely does fuse-only service calls (typically recommends panel upgrade), but the search term is a foot-in-the-door for a customer with a fuse box who likely needs the $1,800-$8,500 panel upgrade. Capturing that search = capturing the lead before they call a competitor.

**How to apply:**
- For each candidate service/page/keyword, ask: (1) does Andrew CAN do this work, (2) does the search intent map to a higher-value job, (3) is the customer profile strong (older home, panel issue, K&T candidate, etc.)
- If yes to (1) + (2 or 3), add it — even if Andrew doesn't market it as a primary line
- The customer search intent may be small ("blown fuse"), but the lead value is large (panel upgrade)
- This applies to GBP services, site service pages, blog/PAA content, and SEO keyword targets
- Don't apply this to services Andrew genuinely can't or won't do (e.g., solar, GC work) — that's no-fake-content territory

**Examples of funnel entry points to keep in mind for P2P:**
- Fuse changing → panel upgrade
- Flickering lights / breaker tripping → diagnostic + likely panel/wiring fix
- "Old electrical" / "1920s home" → K&T conversion
- "EV charger install" → often pairs with panel upgrade
- "Burning smell" / "sparking outlet" → emergency + likely panel/wiring repair
- "Insurance won't cover my home" (older homes) → K&T conversion
