# No licensing the moat

## The rule

Do not propose selling, licensing, consulting on, SaaS-ing, or otherwise distributing to competitors any of the following:

- Ice machine placement intelligence (gap database, scoring, BFR correlation)
- Trades content methodology (article template, voice guide, fact-check rules)
- Multi-tenant content platform codebase
- Content operating system patterns
- SafeBath directory data or senior home safety gap analysis
- Topic sourcer logic or language-specific configurations
- Any proprietary advantage that Bill has built or is building

**The principle:** the moat is owned, not rented. You do not pay your competitors to eat your future locations.

## Why

Bezos, Musk, and every operator with a physical or infrastructural moat does the same thing: build, capture, then consider licensing only to players in markets you will not enter yourself. Tesla did not license Supercharger intelligence. SpaceX did not sell launch placement data. Amazon did not license retail logistics in the first decade. AWS was used internally for years before productization.

Selling placement intelligence to operators in markets Bill is entering is strictly worse than keeping it internal and capturing the locations yourself. The revenue from one $5,000 consulting engagement is less than the lifetime revenue from one $1,500/month machine at a location Bill could have installed.

## How to apply

**Fine:**
- Affiliate relationships (earn commission on sales YOU drive)
- Supplier partnerships (buy hardware from IceRebus, Kooler Ice, etc.)
- Distribution partnerships (Bass Forecast lists YOUR machines on their app)
- Content that ranks and drives affiliate revenue
- Licensing to operators in geographies Bill will NEVER enter (3+ years out, after own market capture)

**Not fine:**
- Consulting engagements selling your data to operators
- SaaS selling your maps or placement scoring
- Open-sourcing your advantage while you are still exploiting it
- "White-labeling" the trades pipeline to other affiliate operators in the same markets
- Selling the multi-tenant platform code to agencies that compete with REI Amplifi's own businesses

## Exception

After Bill has captured the target market in a category (e.g., 150 ice machines across TX/FL/CA by year 5, OR 10 trades × 4 languages ranked on page one), licensing OUT-OF-MARKET becomes acceptable. Never before.
