# Starting is success

## The rule

When Bill is sharing plans, vision, goals, or public commitments, do not add pass/fail criteria, failure tests, or "if we do not hit X by Y date, we failed" language. Share the ambition plainly and confidently.

The only failure state is not starting. Everything else is progress, iteration, and adjustment.

## Why

Bill made this explicit on 2026-04-14 when reviewing draft 7 of a public 12-month plan. He said: "nothing would be a failure unless i didnt start.. i want people to know exacly what my plan is."

Adding failure criteria to a public plan turns it from a declaration of ambition into a contract. Most founder commitments are not contracts. They are direction-setting. Elon's Master Plan, Bezos's shareholder letters, Peter Thiel's Zero to One all share the vision without qualifying it to death.

When Claude adds qualifying language like "at least 8 of 10 series must ship" or "if we do not install 5 machines, this commitment has failed," it:

1. Makes the document smaller and more defensive than Bill wants
2. Lets imperfect execution count as failure even when the business moved forward
3. Treats the public as auditors, not audience
4. Is not how actual founders communicate vision

## How to apply

**When Bill asks for a public plan, commitment, vision document, manifesto, or roadmap:**

- Share the full ambition in future tense
- Describe what will be built, not what "must" be hit
- Name specific moves (ship X, launch Y, operate Z) but not as tests
- Avoid phrases like "hold me accountable to..."
- Avoid phrases like "this commitment has failed if..."
- Avoid phrases like "the failure criteria are..."
- Include what IS within his control (what he will build, what rules he will follow)
- Note what is NOT within his control (market outcomes, client signings, ranking results) without turning those into failure conditions

**When Bill asks for an internal spec or operating document:**

- Here, specific targets and tests are fine
- Internal docs benefit from measurable criteria
- But even then, frame targets as "what we are going for" not "what we must hit to not fail"
