# No done-for-you work (hard rule)

Bill has repeatedly said and shown through experience that hands-on client work does not scale, does not produce lasting customer results, and drains his time. He has a baby arriving, a full portfolio of passive and semi-passive income streams already in motion, and a clear preference for leverage over labor.

**Never pitch Bill on any of the following:**

- Building GHL snapshots for customers
- Setting up GHL accounts for customers
- Audits (SEO, automation, funnel, anything one-time)
- Per-project retainers for implementation
- 1-on-1 coaching
- Hourly consulting
- Managed services (running someone else's account)
- White-glove onboarding
- Custom automation builds
- Any engagement with a customer that requires Bill on a call with them

**The only exception is:**

A GHL client at **$7,500+/month retainer** committed for **12 months minimum** with **deep implementation scope** (treating Bill as embedded, fractional-CMO-style, with real ownership of outcomes inside the client's GHL stack). Anything below that bar is rejected.

**Why:** Bill has tried done-for-you work. Done-for-you customers do not stick with what is built for them. Hatch OM Builder is a recent example (built, customer ghosted). His learned rule is productize or affiliate, never deliver. His time is his scarcest asset, and with a baby arriving the constraint is tighter than ever.

**How to apply: the three-tier customer routing model**

Every customer who asks about GoHighLevel gets routed based on what they actually need. Bill never says "no" to a customer. He routes them to the right partner. This turns the no-DFY rule from a refusal into a routing system that earns affiliate revenue on every tier.

| Tier | What they need | Where to route | REI Amplifi earns |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-service | Just the tool | GHL 30-day trial affiliate link (fp_ref=amplifi-technologies12) | 40% recurring on GHL subscription |
| Help-needed | Setup, onboarding, snapshots, white-label support | Extendly affiliate link (deal=vqzoli) | 20% lifetime on Extendly subs + snapshot sales |
| Enterprise | $7,500+/mo retainer × 12 months, deep implementation, high-net-profit GHL client | Direct to REI Amplifi | Retainer + revenue share + potential equity |

**Applied in content:**

- Every trade post, blog post, and landing page uses this three-tier CTA pattern
  - Primary CTA: GHL affiliate (self-serve)
  - Secondary CTA: "Need it set up for you?" to Extendly (affiliate)
  - Tertiary CTA: Gated enterprise contact form (surfaced only for high-value inbound)
- Every podcast show notes link follows the same pattern
- Every piece of outbound content respects the routing

**Banned CTAs (flag for rewrite if generated):**

- "Book a call" (except on the gated enterprise contact form)
- "Let us set it up for you" (route to Extendly instead)
- "Get 1-on-1 coaching with Bill" (removed — no coaching in the plan)
- "Work with us" (too vague — specify the tier)

**Snapshot sales:** route to Extendly Snapshot Store or SnapshotStore.com as affiliate. Bill does not build snapshots himself.

**When evaluating a new business idea, first filter:** "Can this earn revenue without Bill being on a call with a customer?" If no, drop it.

**Why this works:**

Customers get specialist delivery (Extendly is built for onboarding work). Bill earns affiliate revenue without delivery time. Extendly gets qualified leads. All three parties are better off than they would be with Bill doing the delivery personally at the same price point.
