When working on a paid client deliverable (e.g., Derek's lead-gen site under the fractional CTO contract), don't introduce side investigations of unrelated projects as gates on the deliverable. Specifically: even when a prior project (like globalhighlevel.com getting dinged in SEO) has unresolved questions that could *theoretically* inform the current work, the right move is usually to ship the contracted thing and accept the unknown.

**Why:** Bill is paying $1,500/mo for a specific scope. Side investigations don't move the contracted deliverable forward. The client cares about the website shipping, not about why a different project's SEO went sideways.

**How to apply:**
- When tempted to add an adjacent investigation as a "before we can ship" requirement, pause and ask: is this in the client's contracted scope, or am I projecting risk?
- If it's risk-projection, surface the risk in the plan as a known unknown and proceed.
- Investigations of unrelated projects can become their own engagements but shouldn't block the current build.
- This came up 2026-04-28 when I made "investigate GHL SEO ding" a pre-launch dependency for dereksimkins.com. Bill course-corrected: "stop talking about ghl for this build, were making something for derek, lets focus on his website."
