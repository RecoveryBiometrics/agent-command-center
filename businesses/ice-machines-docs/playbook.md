## Why This Works

The gap is always the same. Stores open at 7-8am. Tournaments launch at 4-5am. The machine does not compete with the store. It serves the hours the store cannot.

Bass Forecast's top three states by app engagement are Texas, Florida, and California. That is 1.5M anglers who plan trips in advance, check conditions before loading the truck, and have smartphones. That is your customer. Not the casual guy buying ice at a gas station. The person who opens an app to check whether it is an Epic day.

The data advantage nobody else has. Bass Forecast Epic day frequency tells you which ramps will have concentrated high-demand weekends months in advance. Kooler Ice and Twice the Ice place machines based on average daily traffic. You place based on peak day concentration. You are at the right ramp before demand spikes. They are still filling out placement applications.

This one change adds 20-30% to annual revenue per machine with zero additional cost. No competitor in ice vending is doing this. IceTalk and V CORE both support remote price adjustment.

## Location Strategy

### Primary Markets — In Order

**1. Florida.** Launch market. Year-round fishing, warm climate, ice sells 12 months. Gulf Coast ramps. Easy permitting on city and county ramps. Lowest seasonal risk.

**2. East Texas Lake Country.** Sam Rayburn, Toledo Bend, Lake Fork, Lake Conroe. Massive bass fishing culture. Brutal summers, ice demand intense. Dense tournament calendar.

**3. California.** Clear Lake, Lake Shasta, Castaic, the Delta. Less tournament culture than south but enormous recreational pressure. Thinner existing ice infrastructure than TX and FL, less competition.

### Location Types — Ranked by Opportunity

**Tier 1 — Best**
- Tournament launch ramps, pre-dawn gap, tournament spike revenue, predictable calendar
- City and county-owned ramps, easiest permits, motivated to provide services
- Weigh-in venues used repeatedly, 30-50 bags in an afternoon, 15-20 times/year, nobody targets these
- Highway corridors 10-15 miles before the lake, "I forgot ice" moment, year-round demand

**Tier 2 — Strong**
- RV parks and campgrounds near fishing lakes, steady 3-5 day stays, families buy daily
- Bait shop exteriors, cover their off-hours without competing with them
- Fishing piers and pay-to-fish ponds, captive audience, zero existing infrastructure

**Tier 3 — Route Filler**
- Boat storage facilities, anglers realize they need ice standing in the parking lot at 6am
- Church-sponsored fishing tournament host ramps, 3-4 events/year per club, relationship play
- Industrial/commercial marinas with mid-week charter traffic

### The Route Model for Low-Volume Locations

Three marginal locations doing 20 bags/day each = 60 bags/day across one route. One weekly service run covers all three. At $2/bag that is $3,600/month, $43,200/year from locations no single-machine operator would bother with. This is how ATM route operators think. Each machine alone is marginal. The route is the business.

### What to Negotiate With Property Owners

**Target:** 10% revenue share for city/county ramps. 15% max for private marinas.
**Never:** Flat monthly lease. Offer revenue share instead, aligns incentives, costs nothing when machine is slow.
**Always:** Multi-year agreement in writing before you place the machine. Never invest $20K+ on a handshake.

### Red Flags to Avoid

- Army Corps of Engineers land, permitting is slow and requires concession agreements
- Rural location with no tournament activity
- Store open 24 hours with ice already inside, you are competing not filling a gap
- No power within 50 feet, utility run adds cost and complexity
- No shade or overhang, machines in direct sun work harder and break faster

## How Customers Pay

Card tap, Apple Pay, Google Pay, credit/debit, cash, coins. Card-first is the right design for your customer: wet hands, pre-dawn, in a hurry. Every card transaction settles to your bank account within 1-2 business days. Every card transaction also gives you timestamp data for BFR correlation. Cash gives you nothing but theft risk.

## How You Get Paid

Card transactions to payment processor (Stripe ~2.7%) to your LLC bank account in 1-2 days. Cash accumulates in the machine and you collect on service visits. Card-first means fewer trips.

Set up a separate LLC bank account per machine cluster from day one. IceTalk and V CORE give you hourly sales data per machine. Match it against costs per location to know which to replicate and which to move.

## The Intelligence Layer — Building the Gap Database

This is the Bezos move. Build the intelligence before you spend a dollar on hardware.

### What You Are Mapping

Every existing ice machine near every major bass lake in Texas, Florida, and California. And more importantly, every ramp where no machine exists.

### Data Sources

| Source | What it gives you | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Kooler Ice locator (koolerice.com/locator-app) | 1,500+ machines in 45 states, all mapped | Free |
| Twice the Ice locator (twicetheice.com/machine-locations) | 3,300+ locations worldwide | Free |
| Ice House America / SmartIce | Their network publicly visible | Free |
| Google Places API | Everything Google knows (names, addresses, hours, lat/long) | ~$32/1,000 lookups (likely free inside $200 monthly credit) |

### The Right Approach — Use Both

Google Places API as primary. Fast, cheap, reliable, structured data. Then manually spot-check the Kooler locator for your top 20 priority lakes. Open the map, zoom in, note what you see. 20 minutes. Costs nothing. Produces data you can stand behind in every conversation.

### Validation Rules — Prevent Bad Data

Tell Claude Code to build these in from day one:

- After every fetch, assert result contains at least N records. If it returns zero or fewer than 10 results for a major state, throw an error, flag it, do not silently write empty rows
- Log every URL fetched, response status code, and record count returned
- Spot-check 5 random results manually after every run, Google the address, confirm the machine exists
- Build a "last verified" column, any row older than 30 days gets flagged
- If scraping JavaScript-rendered locator pages, use Playwright or Selenium, not basic requests

### The Spreadsheet Schema

| Column | What it captures |
|---|---|
| Location name | Marina, ramp, gas station name |
| Address | Full street address |
| Lat / Long | For distance calculations |
| Machine brand | Kooler, Twice the Ice, IHA, unknown |
| Nearest bass lake | Lake name |
| Distance to lake (miles) | Calculated via geopy |
| BFR Epic day frequency | From Bass Forecast data |
| Annual tournament count | From Bassmaster / MLF public calendar |
| Store hours | When do they close, what is the gap |
| Ownership type | City / County / State Park / Private / Army Corps |
| Gap flag | TRUE if no machine within 2 miles of a major ramp |
| Priority score | Epic days + tournament count + gap flag + ownership type |

### Manual Gap Audit — Do This First

Before running any scraper. One weekend. Free. Open Kooler locator + Twice the Ice locator + Google Maps. Pull up top 20 bass lakes in TX, FL, CA one by one. Zoom into every boat ramp. Note: machine present or absent, nearest store and hours, ownership type, estimated weekend traffic.

This gives you verified data you can walk into any conversation with. The scraper automates the monthly refresh. The manual pass gives you the foundation.
